Protection and Dexterity on the Moon

The gloves were designed for Extra-Vehicular Activities (EV) on the Moon and were part of the A7L spacesuit, created through a collaboration between ILC Dover and NASA's David Clark Company. ILC Dover was responsible for the final design and manufacture of the spacesuit, including its multi-layered structure and inflatable elements.

  • Blue silicone rubber fingertips provided sensitivity and dexterity for manipulating tools and equipment on the lunar surface.
  • Human-Centric Design

A7L Apollo Spacesuit

The A7L spacesuit was NASA's primary spacesuit used during the Apollo program. This remarkable piece of engineering protected astronauts from the harsh lunar environment while providing mobility and life support for moonwalks.

  • Multi-layer Protection System
  • Integrated Life Support Backpack
  • Pressurized Joint Systems

Apollo Command/Service Module

The Apollo Command/Service Module (CSM) was the spacecraft that carried astronauts to the Moon and back. This marvel of engineering consisted of two parts: the Command Module for crew housing and reentry, and the Service Module containing propulsion and supplies.

  • Advanced Navigation Systems
  • Reentry Heat Shield Protection
  • Life Support Systems for Long-Duration Spaceflight
